Thesis Summary

Title : A determination of the chemical composition of Alpha Centauri A from strong lines.

Supervisor : Dr. Jim O'Mara, Senior Lecturer

Place : Department of Physics, University of Queensland, Brisbane. Qld - 4072. Australia.

Abstract :

The abundance of iron, magnesium, calcium and sodium in the Alpha Centauri A were determined from the strong lines of these elements having well developed damping wings, using the theory of collisional line broadening of alkali and other spectral lines by neutral hydrogen developed by Anstee & O'Mara (1991, MNRAS, 253:549-560) and the spectrum synthesis program of Dr. John Ross . The abundances in Alpha Centauri A are approximately 0.2 dex higher than in the Sun. The derived abundance is indepedent of non-thermal motions in the photosphere of Alpha Centauri A.

The microturbulence (non-thermal motions) was then derived from the medium-strong lines of iron by fixing the abundance of iron obtained from strong lines of iron and adjusting the microturbulence to match the observed equivalent widths of each line. The microturbulence obtained from medium-strong is 1.34 +/- 0.12 km/s, i.e., 0.20 km/s larger than in the Sun, which is fully consistent with the lower surface gravity of Alpha Centauri A. This microturbulence can then be used to determine the abundance of the elements which have no strong lines in the spectrum.

This method was first time used for Alpha Centauri A. The major problem of using these strong lines for abundance determination in the past was lack of suitable empirical or theoretical damping constants. The tables of line broadening cross-sections from Anstee & O'Mara (1995, MNRAS, 276:859-866) for s-p,p-s transitions helps to obtain damping constants. Anstee, O'Mara & Ross (1997, MNRAS, 284:202-212) have shown that with good f-values for a selection of strong lines it is possible to produce solar iron abundance that is consistent with meteoritic abundances.
